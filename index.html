Social Security Taxation (IRS Pub. 915)

Up to 85% of Social Security income is taxable depending on total "provisional income" = (non-SS income) + SS × 50%.

Gift Tax Rules (2024)

Annual exclusion: $18,000 per recipient per year. Gifts up to this amount require no reporting.

Lifetime exemption: $13,610,000 per person. Gift tax is rarely owed below this cumulative threshold.

Direct tuition payments to educational institutions are excluded from gift tax entirely (any amount).

Medical payments directly to providers are also excluded.

Required Minimum Distributions

Begin at age 73 (SECURE 2.0 Act, 2023). Calculated from the IRS Uniform Lifetime Table. Formula: account balance ÷ life expectancy factor. Applies to Traditional IRA, 401k, 403b, 457b, SEP-IRA. A 25% penalty applies on any shortfall not withdrawn.

Cash Events — Tax Overview

Event typeTax treatment
Inheritance receivedIncome-tax-free to recipient. Inherited investments get a stepped-up cost basis.
House sale (primary)Up to $500k excluded from capital gains (married, lived there 2 of last 5 years).
Business saleCapital gains if stock sale; ordinary income if asset sale. Get a CPA involved.
RSU / equity vestingTaxed as ordinary income (W-2) in the year received.
Insurance payoutLife insurance death benefits are usually tax-free.
Gift receivedNot taxable to recipient. Giver files a return if over $18k/person/year.
Charitable donationDeductible up to 60% of AGI for cash gifts to qualifying charities when itemising.
Legal settlementPhysical injury awards generally tax-free; punitive/employment awards taxable.
Large purchasesNo deduction. Directly reduces account balances.
Home renovationNo immediate deduction. Increases property cost basis, reducing future capital gains.
Moving costsGenerally not deductible for retirees under current law.
These figures are illustrative and based on 2024/25 US tax law. Tax law changes frequently — always verify with a qualified CPA or financial planner before making decisions. This planner uses simplified tax estimates; actual liability will vary based on deductions, credits, filing status, state law, and other factors. Investment returns are assumptions, not guarantees. Sequence-of-returns risk (poor early returns permanently damaging the portfolio) is not modelled.
